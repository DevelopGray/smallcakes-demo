# Staff Card — the site writes checks the counter cashes

Print this (or the print.html version) and keep it by the register.

## The inbox routine

The website promises **"we reply within 1 business day — usually faster."**
- Check the store email at **open, noon, and 4pm**. Every custom request gets
  a same-day reply, even if it's just "got it, quoting tonight."
- Every request has a reference number like **SC-K7M2P9**. Use it in your
  reply and write it on the order ticket — it's how nothing gets lost.
- Customers may **text a photo** to the store number with their reference
  number — match it to the request before quoting.

## The phone promise

The website says **"a real baker answers the phone."** Answer with:
"Smallcakes Clarksville, this is [name]!" If it went to voicemail, call back
within the hour during open hours.

## The wedding script

We don't make tiered wedding cakes. Say it warmly:
> "We don't do tiered wedding cakes — but our cupcake towers and dessert
> tables are showstoppers at receptions, and honestly they're easier on
> your budget. Want me to send photos?"

## The allergen script

Never say "safe," "nut-free," or "gluten-free." Say:
> "Everything is baked fresh in one shared kitchen that regularly uses
> peanuts, tree nuts and gluten, so we can't certify anything allergen-free.
> Let me tell you what's in [flavor] and you can decide what's right for you."

## The review ask (the growth engine)

When someone raves at the counter: "That means a lot — would you mind
leaving us a Google review? The QR on the box takes 30 seconds." Target:
a steady trickle (3–5/week), never a one-day blast.
